26def intersect(key_a: int, mask_a: int, key_b: int, mask_b: int) -> bool: 

27 """ 

28 Return if key-mask pairs intersect (i.e., would both match some of the 

29 same keys). 

30 

31 For example, the key-mask pairs ``00XX`` and ``001X`` both match the keys 

32 ``0010`` and ``0011`` (i.e., they do intersect):: 

33 

34 >>> intersect(0b0000, 0b1100, 0b0010, 0b1110) 

35 True 

36 

37 But the key-mask pairs ``00XX`` and ``11XX`` do not match any of the same 

38 keys (i.e., they do not intersect):: 

39 

40 >>> intersect(0b0000, 0b1100, 0b1100, 0b1100) 

41 False 

42 

43 :param key_a: 

44 :param mask_a: The first key-mask pair 

45 :param key_b: 

46 :param mask_b: The second key-mask pair 

47 :return: True if the two key-mask pairs intersect, otherwise False. 

48 """ 

49 return (key_a & mask_b) == (key_b & mask_a)
